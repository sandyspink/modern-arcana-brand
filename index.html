01 — Brand Story

Where the arcane
meets the grid.

Modern Arcana began as a single Instagram post. One spell. One design. One day at a time. Until we'd covered all 320 spells in the D&D SRD.

The Origin

Modern Arcana was born from a simple creative constraint: what if every D&D spell was treated as subject matter for Swiss International Style graphic design? No dragons. No parchment. No fantasy clichés. Just bold geometry, honest typography, and the rules of the game presented as data worth designing around.

The Mission

D&D has always had incredible world-building, lore, and culture. But the aesthetic surrounding it rarely matches that ambition. Modern Arcana exists to bring genuine design sensibility to that world. To treat the game's mechanics and mythology as worthy of the same craft and intention you'd find in a museum poster or a Helvetica-set annual report. Great design belongs everywhere, including the tabletop.

01
Modernist Rigour
Every design decision is purposeful. No decoration for its own sake. Geometry, typography, and color are the tools. The rules are the material.
02
Encyclopedic Ambition
320 spells. All of them. The completeness of the project is part of its identity. We don't cherry-pick. We commit to the whole system.
03
Wearable Design Objects
A shirt isn't merch. It's a poster you put on your body. Every product should feel like something you'd frame if you weren't wearing it.
02 — Visual References

The tradition
we draw from.

Swiss International Style posters from the 1950s through today. Bold primaries, rigid grids, sans-serif type, and geometric form. This is the visual DNA of Modern Arcana.

03 — Color Palette

Bold primaries.
One dominant.

Modern Arcana draws from the full Swiss Style color tradition: bold, saturated primaries and secondaries used with intention. Arcana Blue is the brand's primary color and the default. The extended palette exists for product design variation across spells and collections, not for decorative use.

Do — Color Usage

  • Default to Arcana Blue for all brand and marketing materials
  • Use extended palette colors for individual spell product designs where the spell's character calls for it
  • Use colors at full saturation. Swiss Style never muddles its palette
  • Pair any palette color with black, white, or each other in flat geometric fields
  • Use white type on all color backgrounds for legibility

Don't — Color Violations

  • Never introduce colors outside the defined palette
  • Never tint, shade, or desaturate any palette color
  • Never use gradients. Every color field is flat and solid
  • Never use more than two colors (plus black and white) in a single composition
  • Never let extended palette colors appear in brand or marketing contexts. They belong to the product layer only
04 — Typography

Helvetica.
Nothing else.

Helvetica is the sole typeface. The definitive grotesque: neutral, authoritative, and inseparable from the Swiss International Style that defines Modern Arcana's visual language. No substitutions.

Type is set left or right aligned depending on the composition. Centered text should be avoided.

✗ Don't
  • Never retype the wordmark in any font. Always use the SVG
  • Never apply drop shadows, strokes, or effects to the wordmark
  • Never use the blue wordmark on a blue background
  • Never place the wordmark on busy photo areas where it becomes illegible. Position over dark or neutral zones
  • Never compress, stretch, or alter the aspect ratio
06 — Product Design Principles

Spells as
design systems.

Each spell card/product follows a strict layout system. The spell is the data. The design is the visualization. Every product is a functional object that also works as a design poster.

01
The Grid is Sacred
Every composition lives on a strict underlying grid. Shapes align to it. Type aligns to it. Even bleed elements break the grid intentionally, not accidentally. The grid is never abandoned. It is stretched.
02
Data Informs Shape
The geometric visualization on each product should feel logically derived from the spell's properties: range, area, damage type, components. Abstract, but not arbitrary. A viewer should be able to make a guess at the spell from the shape alone.
03
Type is Information, Not Decoration
Every piece of type on a product earns its place. Spell name in heavy display type. School and level as small-cap labels. Spell description in tight body copy. No extraneous text. No flavor copy. Just the rules.
04
Geometric Vocabulary
The brand uses three primary shapes: rectangle, circle, and their derived intersections. Triangles and irregular polygons are used sparingly. Shapes should always feel like they were cut from a larger field, not placed on a background.
Rectangle
Primary form. Used for fields, blocks, and crop marks.
Circle
Secondary form. Used cropped, suggesting energy or radius.
Intersection
Core motif. Shapes breaking the frame or overlapping.
Grid Fragment
Two or more shapes in compositional rhythm across the grid.
07 — Photography & Imagery

Real product.
Pure context.

Photography serves the product design, not the other way around. The product is always the hero. Backgrounds are controlled. Lighting is flat or studio-clean. The design on the garment must always be fully legible.

Ad Examples

Eldritch Blast — white garment, neutral background

Magic Missile — black garment, neutral background

Product Shots

Clean studio backgrounds in black, white, or Arcana Blue. Flat-lay or ghost mannequin. The product design must be fully in frame and unobscured. No lifestyle blur. No editorial ambiguity.

Lifestyle Shots

Worn by real people in neutral, minimally distracting environments. Desaturated or near-neutral backgrounds preferred. The garment is always in sharp focus. Avoid strong competing colors in the background.

Campaign Assets

Product shots composited onto full-bleed Arcana Blue or black fields. Text treatment follows the product layout system: heavy Helvetica, uppercase labels. Type is set left or right aligned. Avoid centering text in any composition.

✗ Avoid

Fantasy or medieval aesthetic backdrops: dice, candles, maps, dungeon textures

✗ Avoid

Warm color grading or golden-hour tones. These conflict with the cool, rational palette

✗ Avoid

Heavy photographic filters or dramatic Lightroom edits that shift white balance

✗ Avoid

Props that reference D&D literally. The brand's strength is its refusal to look like typical D&D merch
